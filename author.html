The Author of The Story: "George Orwell"

- The author of the story George Orwell was born in Motihari, India on the 25th of June, 1903.

- His real name was Eric Arthur Blair and he was a writer and a journalist, he's considered one of the most important essayist in the 1930-1940 decade. - Son of Richard Walmsley Blair and Ida Mabel Limouzin, he lived with his mother most of his life and only saw his dad in 1907 when he came to visit. - He studied at the Eton College in the United Kingdom, there he met some future britanic intellectuals like Cyril Connolly. - After graduation from Eton College he joined the Indian Imperial Police in Burma, he worked there for five years and developed a hatred toward Imperialism, this hatred is reflected in one of his first novels; "Burmese Days" which was published in 1934. - The most important George Orwell books are "Down and Out in Paris and London" published in 1933, "Keep the Aspidistra Flying" published in 1936, "1984" which was ironically published in 1948 and finally "Animal Farm" which was published in 1945. - He died on the 21st of January, 1950 and he was buried in Sutton Courtenay, Oxfordshire.
